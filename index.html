Why this work

Open methods, in places where they're rare.

Why open tools
Methodology, not just a result.

Existing analytics in competition robotics, food-bank operations, and adjacent science fields are often proprietary or thin. The lab builds tools that publish the methodology, so the work can be read, audited, and improved by anyone who cares to look.

Who it's for
Practitioners, not just papers.

Competition teams reading the data behind their season. Food-bank operations staff making capacity decisions. Researchers and faculty collaborators reviewing methodology. Students learning to build and reason from real inputs.

What's open vs. held
Tools out, pre-prints private.

Tools, datasets where licensing permits, and methodology are released openly. Pre-publication research, faculty collaborator names, and unpublished findings stay private until peer review. The site shows the surface; the research surfaces when published.

About

A small lab, grounded in published methods.

Null Set Labs is a youth-led research lab. The lab is computational and analytical rather than a wet bench. Its work lives in open-source tools, statistical analysis, and research developed toward publication.

Active research at the lab spans two areas:

  • Robotics. Open-source tools and statistical research for the VEX competition ecosystem, including the live VEX Visualizer dashboard used at Worlds 2026, and concept work on space-systems robotics under the Artemis track.
  • Food Systems and Access. Research and tools spanning nutritional quality classification, community food bank operations, and economic disparities in food access. Public deliverables include the Pantry Dashboard.
How the lab works
  • Statistics serves the question. It runs through both research areas rather than living as a separate one, and is shaped by what each project actually needs to answer.
  • Small and deliberate. New research areas appear only when work exists to justify them.
  • Grounded in published methods. The lab borrows from established literature before inventing, and cites the work it builds on.
  • Open by default. Tools are released with their methodology visible. Research is co-authored with the faculty it is built with.
About Arjun

Arjun Mohanan is a high-school student and the founder of Null Set Labs. He builds tools when he wants to understand a system more deeply, and he collaborates with university faculty to take that work toward publication. His interests sit at the intersection of mathematics, robotics, and applied data science.
